# ClickUp

**Date:** 2026-04-09
**Source:** https://clickup.com
**Verdict:** watch
**Category:** automation
**Relevance Score:** 4

## What is it?

ClickUp is an all-in-one work management platform that combines task management,
collaborative docs, team chat, whiteboards, goal tracking, dashboards, and time
tracking in a single workspace. It markets itself as "The Everything App for
Work," aiming to replace a fragmented stack of tools (Asana, Notion, Slack, a
separate AI assistant) with one unified platform. As of late 2025, it runs on
ClickUp 4.0 (launched December 9, 2025) — its most significant redesign ever,
emphasizing polish and stability over raw feature shipping — and is used by over
4 million teams worldwide including Netflix and McDonald's.

The platform is built around a flexible six-level hierarchy (Workspace → Spaces
→ Folders → Lists → Tasks) and offers 15+ project views including Gantt, Kanban,
Calendar, Timeline, Workload, Mind Map, and Table. Custom fields, custom statuses,
automations, and dependencies let teams model nearly any workflow. The free plan
is genuinely usable (unlimited members and tasks), with paid tiers starting at
$7/user/month (Unlimited) and $12/user/month (Business, billed annually).

ClickUp Brain is their AI add-on — a multi-LLM assistant supporting GPT-4o,
Claude 3.7 Sonnet, and Gemini 2.5 Flash. It provides connected workspace search,
AI-generated task summaries and custom fields, Autopilot Agents for recurring
workflows, an AI Notetaker for meeting transcription (Zoom, Google Meet, Teams),
and plain-English automation building. Brain is a **separate paid add-on** at
approximately $7–9/user/month on top of any base plan.

## Why it matters (or doesn't)

The honest answer for Abonmarche: **not much, right now.** ClickUp is a strong
product, but it largely duplicates capabilities that already exist in your M365
stack — and does so with a weaker integration posture into that stack.

The most critical gap: **there is no native Power Automate connector.** This has
been a community-requested feature for years and was marked "not on roadmap" by
ClickUp's team. Connecting ClickUp to Power Automate requires working through
the ClickUp REST API via HTTP request actions — functional but friction-heavy for
the kind of no-code automation Abonmarche uses across plan review, crash report
processing, and SharePoint workflows. Zapier and Make can bridge the gap, but
that adds yet another layer to manage.

ClickUp does have a native Teams app — tasks unfurl with rich context in channels,
you can create tasks from conversations, and notifications flow into Teams. But
Teams itself is already your communication hub, and the task side of the equation
is covered (at varying quality levels) by Planner and SharePoint task lists. The
value proposition of adding ClickUp is essentially "better project management than
Planner" — which is a legitimate argument, but not a burning need compared to
the AI and GIS priorities on your current roadmap.

ClickUp Brain specifically competes with the Claude Enterprise path you're
evaluating. Paying $7–9/user/month for an AI layer that itself uses Claude 3.7
under the hood makes little sense when you're building toward native Claude
integration via MCP and the Agent SDK.

There **is** one genuine angle worth noting: ClickUp has engineering firm
templates and use cases around tracking CAD files, compliance deadlines,
multi-discipline team coordination, and regulatory inspection schedules — all
real Abonmarche problems. If the Digital Solutions team ever outgrows Planner
for managing the AI CoP initiative portfolio, infrastructure project pipelines,
or cross-team delivery tracking, ClickUp would be worth revisiting.

## Technical details

- **Pricing:** Free Forever (limited); Unlimited $7/user/mo (annual); Business
  $12/user/mo (annual); Enterprise (custom, requires sales call)
- **ClickUp Brain add-on:** ~$7–9/user/mo on top of base plan; workspace-wide
  requirement means you pay for every seat even if only some users need AI
- **Billing gotcha:** Assigning a task to a contractor/guest can silently upgrade
  them to a paid "member" — some teams have seen bills jump 10x overnight
- **Microsoft integrations:** Native Teams app (task creation, unfurling,
  notifications); SharePoint via API/webhooks; **no native Power Automate
  connector**
- **AI models:** GPT-4o, Claude 3.7 Sonnet, Gemini 2.5 Flash (multi-LLM)
- **Security:** SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA (Enterprise tier); does not train on customer
  data; UpGuard A-rating (822/950)
- **Maturity:** Very mature (founded 2017, 4M+ teams); ClickUp 4.0 is a
  stabilization release focused on reducing historic bugginess and performance
  complaints
- **Limitations:** Known performance issues in large workspaces; steep learning
  curve for customization; automations less flexible than dedicated tools; no
  ArcGIS integration; no direct MCP server support

## Recommendation

**Watch** — check back if one of these conditions changes:

1. **Power Automate connector ships.** ClickUp has a substantial government/
   municipal customer base that needs this. If they ship a certified Power
   Automate connector, the integration story for Abonmarche changes materially.
2. **M365 Planner continues to disappoint.** If the Digital Solutions team's
   project tracking needs outgrow what Planner + Teams Tasks can handle,
   ClickUp at the Unlimited tier ($7/user/mo for a small team) is a credible
   upgrade with engineering-specific templates.
3. **ClickUp Brain adds MCP support.** ClickUp Brain's connected search is
   interesting, but it's a walled garden today. If they open it to MCP-style
   tool connections, it could slot into a broader agent workflow.

For now, don't pursue a pilot. The lack of a native Power Automate connector,
the redundancy with M365 tools already in place, and the conflict with the
Claude Enterprise investment path all argue against adding ClickUp to the stack.
